Spotlight on Our Students


Our students tend to be very eager to learn. However, there are a few who only come because they must pass the tough English tests in the public schools. While they learn English in school the focus is primarily on listening and reading skills, they have few opportunities in the large public school classrooms to speak, and rarely write more than a few words and phrases. This is the main reason they come to us. Oral and writing skills are very important when learning language, so we offer Native speaking foreign teachers, and emphasize the verbal and written side of language learning. Don’t misunderstand, we teach ALL four skills but we focus on the oral and writing because those are the skills these students don’t practice enough.


Our students range from 3 years to college age, and even some adults. They may come with zero language or advance language skills, it’s our job to place them in the right class, at the right level, and then watch them grow. Students are placed by their language level NOT their age, so it’s common to see mixed ages in our younger classes. For older students we place them by level however, it’s still common to see mixed ages.


The one thing to remember about our students is that from a young age they have tremendous pressure on them to get “good” grades. Many of our students spend their entire weekend taking “extra” courses to improve their grades and their chances are becoming “top” students. The higher they score the better their chances are at getting into a first-rate college in the future. Testing is the rule in China and it begins early. They will first test into a good primary school, then test again to get into a top-notch middle school, and test a third time to get into the best high school, and finally, the most important test of their lives, the Gaokao (college entrance exam) which determines which college they can attend. Most Chinese students work very hard, and they are pushed along by worried parents who fret about their future. For this reason at Perfect English our job is to make the learning fun, and not full of pressure.


Our students learn in a fun, positive, and engaging language environment. We teach by encouraging the process and in most of our classes we strive for more student-talk time verses teacher-talk time. This system pays off and our students are some of the best English speakers in the city, that isn’t bragging, their test scores prove it.

Interested in teaching English in China? Go ahead and reach out on our contact page. We'll be pleased to answer you within a few hours.
