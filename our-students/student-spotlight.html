Spotlight on Our Students


Our students come to improve their English skills because they have limited opportunities in the public schools to do this. Chinese public schools focus primarily on listening and reading skills as classes are often too large to accommodate oral and writing skills. Oral and writing skills are very important when learning language, so we offer Native speaking foreign teachers, and emphasize the verbal and written side of language learning. Don’t misunderstand, we teach ALL four skills but we focus on the oral and writing because those are the skills these students don’t practice enough.


Our students range from 3 years to college age, and even some adults. They may come with zero language or advance language skills, it’s our job to place them in the right class, at the right level, and then watch them grow. Students are placed by their language level NOT their age, so it’s common to see mixed ages in all our classes.


Chinese students have tremendous pressure on them to get “good” grades. Many of our students spend their entire weekend taking “extra” courses to improve their grades and are pushed to become “top” students. The higher they score the better their chances are at getting into a first-rate college in the future. Testing is the rule in China and it begins early. They first test into a good primary school, then test again to get into a top-notch middle school, and test a third time to get into the best high school, and finally, the most important test of their lives, the Gaokao (college entrance exam) which determines which college they can attend. Most Chinese students work very hard, and they are pushed along by worried parents who fret about their future. For this reason at Perfect English our job is to make the learning fun, and not full of pressure.


Our students learn in a fun, positive, and engaging language environment. We teach by encouraging the process and in our classes we strive for more student-talk time verses teacher-talk time. This system pays off and our students are some of the best English speakers in the city, that isn’t bragging, their test scores prove it.

Spotlight Student: Solomon

Here is one of our top students, meet Solomon (Zhou Le) his story is unique in some ways but fairly typical of many Chinese kids.

Solomon had been a pretty good student until he went to middle school. He was with other top students but he found himself struggling. When it was time to take the exam for entering high school he didn’t do well. He managed to get into a good school but only because he could play the viola. In high school he continued to struggle and finally decided to leave the Chinese educational system. He opted to attend a private high school but the level of education wasn’t good. Finally, he came to Perfect English and joined our College Prep Program. In one year, he managed to improve his English to the point where he took English 1A (on-line) from a Community College in the US and passed with the highest score in the class. He then enrolled at College of Alameda to complete a 2-year program and attain his Associate Science degree in Business Management. He is about to transfer to complete his Bachelor’s degree in Business. He went from failing in the Chinese system to thriving in the “western” system of education.
