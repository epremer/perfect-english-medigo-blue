Teaching In China

Teaching in China can be exciting and rewarding, but it requires you to be flexible. China’s laws are very strict so the process for getting here is challenging. However if you have what it takes the rewards are worth it.

Teaching in China is an adventure, but like most adventures it will require planning on your part. The process has several steps and each one will require you to wait, however, if you plan ahead you can make this process a little shorter. Currently you can ONLY teach English in China if you are a Native speaker from one of the following countries: USA, Canada, UK, Ireland, Australia, or New Zealand. We are able to hire from South Africa, but it will depend on your educational background.

If you are at least 22 years old (and not over 58) plus have a Bachelors degree (in anything) this is what you need to do:

1. You need a current passport (from your country of birth) and it must have at least 6 months remaining and 2 blank pages for visas.

2. You need a passport size headshot with a white background.

3. You need a 1 page resume (CV) no longer than 1 page please. It should contain your contact information, address, phone, email, skype, etc. You need to list your education (BA or BS is mandatory) and TEFL or TESOL (120 hours minimum) and any other education you have after high school. Then list only the last 3 jobs you have done that include teaching or training skills.

If you have all of the above then proceed to step 4:

4. Your Bachelor Degree (BA or BS) in anything, this will need to be notarized, apostilled, and finally stamped by the Chinese embassy closest to where you live.

5. Your TEFL or TESOL Certification, this will need to be notarized, apostilled, and finally stamped by the Chinese embassy closest to where you live.

6. A criminal background check from your local police, this too needs to be notarized, apostilled, and stamped by the Chinese embassy closest to where you live.

When you have gathered items 4-6, you should plan your first trip to the local Chinese Embassy to get them stamped. (see photo)

7. You need to complete the Health Check form that the employer sends to you and have that notarized.

When you have sent the employer items 1-7, then you need to wait until the Chinese government issues your letter of invite (LOI). When the employer notifies you that the LOI has been sent to your local Chinese embassy, you will make a second trip there. This time you need to bring your LOI and proof you have purchased your airline ticket to China. Please do not book a ticket until you have discussed the process further with your employer. This is a time sensitive process so it is important you purchase your ticket accordingly.

If everything is in order you will make your third and final trip back to the Chinese embassy to pick up your passport with your 30 day Z-Visa. Now it’s time to pack and go.

Things to Know Before You Go!

Do not come to China to work unless you have a Z-Visa. It is illegal to work in China on any other type of visa and you can be deported, or jailed, and fined. Anyone who offers you a job without a valid Z-visa isn’t a legal employer so; don’t be scammed.

Do not come unless you have a valid offer of employment in writing that states a salary and other terms.

Do your homework and make sure the company is solid and has good references. Make sure where they are located in China and that they have the proper business license to hire teachers from the government. Ask around on Teaching English websites then decide.

Don’t base your choice on salary or location, both can be very misleading. Salaries will depend on the location, larger cities cost more for basic living expenses so be wise. Locations can vary and it’s really up to what type of Chinese adventure you want.

Perfect English has been in business over 16 years and we have licenses to place teachers in our own schools and in public schools. We are located in a growing 3rd tier city which means while our salaries are competitive for this area they are not competitive with 1st tire cities like Beijing or Shanghai. However, we have a low cost of living and still offer many Western conveniences. We have a wonderful transportation system and an International airport with easy access by train or taxi. Our location is suitable for making trips to Korea, Japan, or even anywhere in China. We are 8 hours by train from Beijing which means you could plan a short trip on your days off. We are in the Northeast of China which means cold winters, but mild summers. We have a lot to offer if you decide to choose us for your China adventure!
